# Curious Speaking
## Founder's Edition
### Master Unit 07 — Libraries

**Theme:** Culture
**Driving Question:** Why do libraries still matter?

# Pattern Training

## What
| Step | Sentence |
|---|---|
|1|Libraries collect knowledge.|
|2|Libraries collect knowledge for everyone.|
|3|Libraries collect knowledge for everyone in many different forms.|
|4|Libraries collect knowledge for everyone in many different forms, from printed books to digital resources.|

## Why
| Step | Sentence |
|---|---|
|1|Libraries are important.|
|2|Libraries are important because they provide free access.|
|3|Libraries are important because they provide free access to information.|
|4|Libraries are important because they provide free access to information and lifelong learning opportunities.|

## How
| Step | Sentence |
|---|---|
|1|People visit libraries.|
|2|People visit libraries to study.|
|3|People visit libraries to study and do research.|
|4|People visit libraries to study and do research in a quiet place where they can focus.|

## What if
| Step | Sentence |
|---|---|
|1|Libraries might disappear.|
|2|Libraries might disappear from some cities.|
|3|Libraries might disappear from some cities if people stop using them.|
|4|Libraries might disappear from some cities if people stop using them, reducing equal access to knowledge.|

# Automatic Response
1. Why are libraries important? → They provide free access to information.
2. What can people do there? → They can study and do research.
3. Who can use libraries? → Everyone.
4. What might happen without libraries? → Equal access to knowledge could decrease.

# Expansion Challenge
| Step | Sentence |
|---|---|
|1|A library opens doors.|
|2|A library opens doors to learning.|
|3|A library opens doors to learning for every generation.|
|4|A library opens doors to learning for every generation, regardless of age or background.|

# Final Speaking
Libraries collect knowledge in many different forms, from printed books to digital resources. They are important because they provide free access to information and lifelong learning opportunities. People visit libraries to study and do research in a quiet place where they can focus. Without libraries, equal access to knowledge could become more difficult.
