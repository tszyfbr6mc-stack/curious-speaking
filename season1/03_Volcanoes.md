# Curious Speaking
## Founder's Edition
### Master Unit 03 — Volcanoes

**Theme:** Earth Science  
**Driving Question:** What is the difference between magma and lava?

---

# Pattern Training

| Step | Sentence |
|---|---|
|1|Volcanoes release melted rock.|
|2|Volcanoes release melted rock during eruptions.|

# Automatic Response

1. Where is magma found?  
→ It is found underground.

2. When does magma become lava?  
→ After it reaches the surface.

3. Why do volcanoes erupt?  
→ Because pressure keeps increasing.

4. What might people do during an eruption?  
→ They might leave the area quickly.

# Final Speaking

Volcanoes release melted rock during eruptions from deep underground after pressure builds for many years. Pressure keeps increasing because magma rises and trapped gas cannot escape easily. When magma reaches the surface, it becomes lava and slowly cools into solid rock. Scientists monitor volcanoes every day to warn nearby communities before an eruption.
