# Curious Speaking
## Founder's Edition
### Master Unit 04 — Dinosaurs

**Theme:** Ancient Life  
**Driving Question:** How do fossils help us understand dinosaurs?

---

# Pattern Training

## What

| Step | Sentence |
|---|---|
|1|Dinosaurs lived on Earth.|
|2|Dinosaurs lived on Earth millions of years ago.|
|3|Dinosaurs lived on Earth millions of years ago in many different environments.|
|4|Dinosaurs lived on Earth millions of years ago in many different environments before they became extinct.|

## Why

| Step | Sentence |
|---|---|
|1|Scientists study fossils.|
|2|Scientists study fossils to learn about dinosaurs.|
|3|Scientists study fossils to learn about dinosaurs that no longer exist.|
|4|Scientists study fossils to learn about dinosaurs that no longer exist and the world they lived in.|

## How

| Step | Sentence |
|---|---|
|1|Bones become fossils.|
|2|Bones become fossils over a long time.|
|3|Bones become fossils over a long time under layers of rock.|
|4|Bones become fossils over a long time under layers of rock as minerals slowly replace them.|

## What if

| Step | Sentence |
|---|---|
|1|People might discover a fossil.|
|2|People might discover a fossil in the future.|
|3|People might discover a fossil in the future that changes what we know.|
|4|People might discover a fossil in the future that changes what we know about dinosaur life.|

# Automatic Response

1. When did dinosaurs live?  
→ Millions of years ago.

2. Why do scientists study fossils?  
→ To learn about dinosaurs.

3. How do bones become fossils?  
→ Minerals slowly replace them.

4. What might a new fossil do?  
→ It might change what we know.

---

# Expansion Challenge

| Step | Sentence |
|---|---|
|1|Every fossil tells a story.|
|2|Every fossil tells a story about the past.|
|3|Every fossil tells a story about the past that scientists can study.|
|4|Every fossil tells a story about the past that scientists can study to understand ancient life.|

---

# Final Speaking

Dinosaurs lived on Earth millions of years ago in many different environments before they became extinct. Scientists study fossils to learn about dinosaurs and the world they lived in. Bones become fossils under layers of rock as minerals slowly replace them. A new fossil discovery could change what we know about ancient life.
